Having the right certificate can make all the difference. But with so many out there, getting the right one isn’t easy. That’s where OpenAI Academy comes in.
OpenAI, the company behind the ChatGPT models, has introduced a learning platform through its OpenAI academy that offers AI courses for upskilling professionals. These courses cover topics like AI fundamentals, Applied AI, Agents, and Workflows.
The best part? All of these courses are completely free and offer certificate of completion after finishing the final assessment.
This article lists the best OpenAI Academy’s AI courses available and explains whom each course is best suited for.
AI Foundations | For the basics
This course acts as a quick introduction to AI, large language models, and ChatGPT.
A beginner-friendly course that focuses on using AI in everyday work. It covers prompting, adding context, reviewing outputs, and using AI responsibly. Instead of heavy theory, the course helps learners build confidence with AI tools.
What makes this course special?
Best for learners who are new to AI and want to understand the basics before using it at work.
Applied AI Foundations | For the workflows
This course is for learners who already know the basics of AI and want to apply it better at work.
A practical course that focuses on turning repeated tasks into repeatable workflows. It shows how to break work into steps, use ChatGPT at the right points, and add review checkpoints. The course is less about prompts and more about making AI useful in actual work.
What makes this course special?
Best for professionals who want to use AI for recurring workplace tasks.
Agents and Workflows | For agentic work
A focused course that teaches how to direct agents through structured tasks. It covers giving context, setting boundaries, defining outputs, reviewing drafts, and improving workflows. The course is useful for learners who are already comfortable using AI and want to move beyond basic prompting.
What makes this course special?
Best for learners who want to understand how agents can support structured work.

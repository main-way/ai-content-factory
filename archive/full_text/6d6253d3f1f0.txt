Sharon He, Co-founder and CEO of Weeknd, tells CB Insights how they view the market, customer needs, and their company.
How do you define your market and where does your company fit into that space?
Weeknd is a TikTok for local events for users, but what we really do is support event hosts. The biggest problem right now is that a lot of people are hosting local events out of their own passions and hobbies, and they’re not getting paid for it. Often, it’s fun in the beginning, but after a while, it gets exhausting, and there’s no way on any of the platforms, like Luma or Meetup, to help hosts monetize their events. Where we come in is that we provide a lot of support to hosts, and we look for sponsors, particularly in the CPG (consumer packaged goods) market. That’s everyday stuff that you see in retail stores and grocery stores. For these brands, Instagram or other social media ads don’t work that well because nobody can taste or feel the products. We connect sponsors to hosts and offer them an in-person taste experience at our events. Brands can reach 200 to 500 people at these local events, and people can taste and feel the products right away and often make a purchase decision on the spot. It also gives event hosts ways to monetize their events instead of just paying out of their own pocket for expenses and spending all the time and energy to put together an event for the local community. It’s great work that they’re doing, but there’s just not an app out there that gives that support to them currently.

Max Grossenbacher, CEO of Resilient, tells CB Insights how they view the market, customer needs, and their company.
How do you define your market and where does your company fit into that space?
The global corporate wellness market is about $65B and is projected to reach $130B in 2034. We used Switzerland, one of the hardest enterprise markets to crack, as our beachhead, before expanding into the US. In Switzerland alone, burnout costs employers up to 10B Swiss francs a year. In the US, this number is currently estimated at around $300B a year. Chronic stress itself, according to Pete Schnell, costs the US over $1T a year. We differentiate through our exclusive licensed tool, STOA, combined with wearables and AI coaching. Competitors like WellHub and nilo.health are either very generic or reactive. And what we do is detect burnout before it becomes a crisis. We actually start to detect chronic stress very early on. This is our strongest suit. We also partnered very closely with Swiss psychiatrists, psychologists, and psychotherapists to draw on this extensive experience and provide the right type of support to employees when needed. We combine this with a lot of different data types. We have our own data fusion algorithms that can detect patterns in multi-model data streams to effectively predict and prevent burnout or the chronification of stress.

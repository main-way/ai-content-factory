Title:Confidence Score Guided Incremental and Speaker Adaptive Pseudo-Labeling for Semi-Supervised Elderly Speech Recognition
View PDF HTML (experimental)Abstract:This paper proposes a novel confidence score guided incremental and speaker adaptive pseudo-labeling approach for semi-supervised elderly speech recognition. It facilitates higher-quality pseudo-label selection and progressive refinement, while also mitigating speaker heterogeneity. A confidence estimation module is designed to rank the reliability of untranscribed data, enabling a curriculum learning trajectory that progressively folds in unlabeled data subsets from high to low confidence. Speaker-specific characteristics are captured through speaker adaptive training with learnable prompts. Experiments on the English DementiaBank Pitt and Cantonese JCCOCC MoCA elderly speech datasets suggest that the proposed method outperforms the semi-supervised baseline using no confidence scores guided incremental or speaker adaptive pseudo-labeling by statistically significant word error rate (WER) or character error rate (CER) reductions of 1.45% and 2.27% absolute (6.21% and 6.98% relative).

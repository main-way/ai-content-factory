Malcolm Bouzi, Founder and CEO of Rapchr, tells CB Insights how they view the market, customer needs, and their company.
How do you define your market and where does your company fit into that space?
We define our market across three levels. Global music consumption, hip-hop audiences, and highly engaged hip-hop culture participants. Our total adjustable market will be global music fans, with streaming alone, which is led by hip-hop, projected to reach about $131B in revenue by 2030. Our serviceable available market is hip-hop fans, who are one of the most commercially influential cultural segments globally. Our serviceable obtainable market would be hip-hop aficionados, who would be the most engaged listeners, representing about 17% of the fanbase, and roughly $85B in annual spend. We compete with streaming, social, and music discussion platforms, but our advantage is turning fan opinion into measurable cultural data that changes over time. We differentiate by measuring structured fan sentiment and cultural perception, not just listening behavior.

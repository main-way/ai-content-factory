Joel Sellam, CEO if Stargo, tells CB Insights how they view the market, customer needs, and their company.
How do you define your market and where does your company fit into that space?
We operate in the global freight logistics and supply chain market, one of the largest and most complex industries in the world. While trillions of dollars flow through this ecosystem, most day-to-day operations still depend on unstructured and semi-structured data, emails, PDFs, contracts, spreadsheets, EDI (electronic data interchange), messages, and the constant human communication between shippers, forwarders, carriers, and partners. This is the industry’s core problem; traditional systems like TMS (transportation management systems), ERP (enterprise resource planning), and visibility platforms were built for structured data and predefined processes. They work well in theory, but they break down in real-world operations where information is incomplete, delayed, inconsistent, or human-generated. As a result, even highly digitized organizations remain heavily manual, slow to react, expensive to operate, and difficult to scale.
